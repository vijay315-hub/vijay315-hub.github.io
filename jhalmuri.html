Discover India’s viral Bengali street food loved for its spicy taste, mustard oil flavor and Kolkata culture.

About Jhalmuri

Jhalmuri is one of the most popular street foods in West Bengal. Made using puffed rice, mustard oil, onions, chilli and spices, this snack is famous across India for its unique Bengali flavor.

Many travelers visiting Kolkata enjoy eating fresh Jhalmuri from local street vendors near railway stations and markets.

Traditional Bengali Jhalmuri Recipe

Jhalmuri is a quick and spicy street food from West Bengal. It is famous in Kolkata railway stations, beaches and local markets. The special mustard oil flavor makes this snack unique and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups puffed rice (murmura)
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 1 chopped tomato
  • 1 green chilli
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard oil
  • Chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice

How To Make Jhalmuri

  1. Take puffed rice in a large bowl.
  2. Add chopped onion, tomato and green chilli.
  3. Add coriander leaves and spices.
  4. Pour mustard oil and lemon juice.
  5. Mix everything quickly.
  6. Serve immediately for crunchy taste.

Why Bengali Jhalmuri is Famous?

The spicy taste, mustard oil aroma and crunchy texture make Jhalmuri one of the most loved street foods in India. Tourists visiting Kolkata often try authentic Bengali Jhalmuri.
